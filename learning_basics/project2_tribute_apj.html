A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Here are a few moments from his life...

  • Born on 15 Oct 1931 in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from the University of Madras.
  • 1960: Graduated in Aerospace Technology from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960: Joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the DRDO.
  • 1969: Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1970s: Directed the Project Devil and Project Valiant.
  • 1982-83: Appointed as the Chief Executive of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP).
  • 1992-99: Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the DRDO.
  • Served as the Chief Project Coordinator during the testing phase of Pokaran-II.
  • 2002-07: Served as 11th President of India.
  • Honoured with Padma Bhushan(1981) and Padma Vibhushan(1990) for his work with ISRO and DRDO and his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.
  • 1997: Honoured with India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna.
